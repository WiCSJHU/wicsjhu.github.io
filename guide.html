Welcome to WiCS: A Tiny Guide

00/ hello

Women in CS (WiCS) is for women/non-binary individuals, as well as other underrepresented groups in tech (and allies!), who are involved in CS (whether it's majoring, minoring, or seriously considering majoring/minoring).

We focus on:

  • forming friendships
  • helping you find internships and jobs
  • providing a welcoming place to both work and hang out (and eat!)

Our events often include:

  • company tech talks/workshops
  • movie nights
  • dinners with WiCS alumnae
  • breakfast study sessions
  • student-led workshops/talks

and always include:

  • Coding Circle, a weekly meeting (Friday, Malone 107, 4:30pm) with snacks, nail polish, and playing cards. We'll discuss the Topic of the Week ("internship hunting," "group project tips") and also just hang out and unwind.
  • Our mentorship program, where we pair underclassmen and upperclassmen (based on their interests). Mentors get to share their experiences and offer advice; mentees get to ask questions and receive helpful tips; and everyone gets to meet new people and participate in fun bonding events. Sign-ups are in the winter/spring and for active members only!

01/ be in the loop

Two main things:

  • join our Facebook group, where we post about WiCS events
  • join our mailing list, where we forward job/internship opportunities as well as info on relevant events

You can also (but don't have to):

  • like our Facebook page, where we post photos from events and (starting in Sept 2018) announce WiCS Members of the Month
  • check out the department's page on WiCS, which links to some additional resources

02/ be a member

Everyone is welcome to join our group/mailing list and attend our general events, but members get:

  • membership status (if you like bragging rights), plus their name on our website
  • access to our database of resources (for internships, scholarships, etc.)
  • access to the ACM/WiCS lounge (in Malone Hall)

and active members additionally get:

  • invites to exclusive company events
  • our mentorship program (you must be an active member this Fall 2018 in order to sign up for the 2018-19 school year)

To become a member of WiCS, come to three of our events. During/after your third event, let Vivian or Cindy (on the exec board) know and one of them will add you to our site and give you database/lounge access.

To remain an active member of WiCS, come to at least two events each semester. (Remind Vivian or Cindy during/after your second event to get your name on the active members list for the current semester!)

03/ about acm

Membership in WiCS automatically grants you membership in the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM), the other undergraduate CS organization on campus.

Note: ACM is a more "general CS" group--less discussion of interview tips, workplace environments, etc. and more speakers who talk about specialized CS fields (including speakers from our CS department!).

WiCS and ACM collaborate on a ton of events, including:

  • game nights (both board and video)
  • The OlympiCS, which is a department-wide day of CS games (i.e., students and faculty competing for prizes)
  • TBA: a student-led workshop series on things like Git and Python this fall!

These joint events will appear on WiCS event calendars. However, ACM meetings (every Thursday evening) will not--check the Google Calendar on our events page (and/or follow the ACM's Facebook page) to stay tuned for those.

Feel free to ask our WiCS exec board about ACM!
