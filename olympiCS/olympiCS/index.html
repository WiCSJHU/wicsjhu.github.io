FAQ

What kind of prizes? Prizes like random, miscellaneous swag. Also, grand prizes like free dinner/lunch with CS faculty members of your choice!

What kind of games? Games like Pictionary, Wikipedia races, and our grand finale: algorithm dancing. Plus a few more surprises.

I can't stay for the whole time. That's okayâ€”drop by and participate in a few games anyway! You still have to sign up (above) if you want to do this. (Also, note that you're not eligible for prizes unless you're there the whole time. Sorry.)

I want to participate in the games. Great! Fill out the corresponding sign-up form above. You can list team members (up to teams of 4) or sign up as an individual (in which case we'll find additional team members for you).

I want to help out. We need volunteers to help plan/run games (these people can't compete) and volunteers to help with set-up/tear-down (these people CAN compete). Email us at jhucsgames@gmail.com and let us know which kind of volunteer you'd like to be.

Who runs this thing? The OlympiCS is hosted by JHU's undergraduate CS groups: ACM (Association of Computing Machinery), WiCS (Women in CS), and UPE (Upsilon Pi Epsilon).
