FAQ

What kind of prizes? Prizes like random, free swag. Also, grand prizes like free dinner or lunch with CS faculty members of your choice!

What kind of games? Games like Pictionary, Wikipedia races, and our grand finale: algorithm dancing. Plus a few more surprises.

I can't stay for the whole time. That's okay—drop by and participate in a few games anyway! You still have to sign up if you want to do this. (Also, note that you're not eligible for prizes unless you're there the whole time. Sorry.)

I'm a faculty/staff member. Do I bring my family? Yes! Your spouse (and children, if they're old enough for things like Jeopardy) can compete on your team. We'll also have kids' games for those too young to compete (see next question).

What kind of kids' games? Drawing, water balloons, relay races, and (we're working on) a bouncy castle. Plus, they'll get to help judge some of the main games.

But I'm brand-new to CS/forget how mergesort works/etc. No worries—you can still compete! The OlympiCS is CS-themed, but it's a bonding event, not a test (think "draw your favorite professor" and "guess how many people like C" rather than "prove O(n log n) time"). Read: no studying involved. Also, we'll give review sheets out before algo dancing!

I want to participate in the games. Great! Fill out the corresponding sign-up form above. You can list team members (<=4 per team) or sign up as an individual.

How do sign-ups and teams work? The OlympiCS is designed for teams of 4, and all participants must sign-up in advance.

Students:

  1. Sign up as a team <=4. Only one person on your team needs to do this.

  2. If you're an individual sign-up or a team < 4, we'll match you up with additional people for game day.

  3. If you need to change teams, add/change team members, etc., contact us at jhucsgames@gmail.com.

Staff:

  1. Sign up and indicate all family members who will be attending.

    • You can request that your competing family member(s) be on the same team OR not be on the same team (if you'd like to compete against your spouse, or your child wants to compete with undergrads, etc.).

    • Note whether or not any of your child(ren) will be participating in the kids' games.

    • Request other staff members for your team.

  2. If you're an individual sign-up or your total team < 4, we'll match you up with additional staff members for game day.

  3. If you need to change teams, add/change team members, etc., contact us at jhucsgames@gmail.com.

I want to help out. We need volunteers to help plan/run games (these people can't compete) and volunteers to help with set-up/tear-down (these people CAN compete). Email us at jhucsgames@gmail.com and let us know which kind of volunteer you'd like to be.

I have another question/I want to edit my sign-up or team info. Email us at jhucsgames@gmail.com!

Who runs this thing? The OlympiCS is hosted by JHU's undergraduate CS groups: ACM (Association of Computing Machinery), WiCS (Women in CS), and UPE (Upsilon Pi Epsilon).
